2 Why You Need a Web Service Now

Japan's cruise market is the largest in Asia, with over 3 million passengers annually. Major cruise lines such as MSC, Royal Caribbean, and Princess continue to expand Japan-originating routes, and as online booking demand surges rapidly, only travel agencies with a web service can maintain competitiveness.

Building cruise product pages one by one requires enormous time and cost. It is difficult to respond quickly when schedules or fares change, and introducing all products from multiple cruise lines is virtually impossible — limiting customer choice. CruiseLink solves all of these problems.

3 Challenges You Face

❶ High Barriers to Building a Cruise Website

Cruise websites have a completely different structure from regular travel sites. Integrating different APIs and booking systems for each cruise line requires massive initial development costs, and even after development is complete, ongoing monthly maintenance fees are not negligible. Finding developers with cruise domain expertise is extremely difficult.

❷ Endless Content Creation

You need to manually collect, translate, and register schedule, fare, and port-of-call information for 9 cruise lines and hundreds of cruise products — 2 to 4 hours per product. The inability to reflect changes quickly means you may miss sales opportunities.

❸ Lead Loss During Nights & Holidays

Failing to respond to inquiries outside business hours causes customers to defect to competitors. AI-powered 24-hour customer support prevents churn, and LINE booking inquiries can be automatically connected.
